# Auto-grill record

## Strongest findings

- The archive mixed GitHub stars, estimated growth, social comments, article mentions, and editorial importance under “popularity,” even though the units are not comparable.
- Several growth claims lacked stored historical snapshots.
- Community totals lacked stable post URLs/IDs, so they could not be reproduced.
- The latest Graphify description incorrectly generalized code-only local processing to documents and media.
- The Ponytail report repeated an obsolete 80–94% flat reduction claim after the project published a corrected mean of about 54% with a per-task maximum of 94%.
- Security and market-position language sometimes converted project claims or secondary articles into established fact.

## Revisions

The archive and template now use an evidence-labeled editorial ranking, require observation times and primary sources, distinguish project claims from independent verification, and record coverage gaps. The 2026-07-22 report received targeted primary-source corrections. Earlier reports are explicitly marked `LEGACY_UNVERIFIED` in the methodology until reconstructed.

## Residual risk

This pass did not independently reproduce every benchmark, social metric, or item in every historical report. Stars and repository contents change continuously. Source existence does not prove the source's claim. See the porting decisions in `METHODOLOGY.md`.
